## Article 1 (Priority 1)
**Title:** How Much Does It Cost to Build a Cannabis Extraction Lab in 2026?
**URL:** /insights/cannabis-extraction-lab-cost-2026
**Target:** 1,000+ words
**Target query:** "cost extraction lab cannabis", "cannabis extraction facility buildout cost"

### Outline

**Introduction (100w)**
The cost range for a cannabis extraction facility spans from $150,000 for a small-scale ethanol operation to $2M+ for a full-scale hydrocarbon facility with CO2 backup. The variance is that wide because the variables are that numerous. This guide breaks down every cost component so you can build a realistic budget before you commit to a site or equipment.

**H2: The Major Cost Categories**

**H3: Real Estate and Build-Out**
- C1D1 classified room construction: $50-150/sqft depending on jurisdiction and finish level
- HVAC systems sized for solvent loads: $30,000-150,000 depending on capacity
- Electrical: 3-phase power, panel upgrades, explosion-proof fixtures
- Plumbing: solvent storage, floor drains, eyewash stations
- Fire suppression: FM200, dry chemical, or sprinkler depending on AHJ requirements
- Permitting and inspection fees: $5,000-50,000 depending on state and municipality

**H3: Extraction Equipment**
- Entry-level hydrocarbon closed-loop system: $15,000-40,000
- Mid-range hydrocarbon system (5-10 lb capacity): $60,000-120,000
- Commercial hydrocarbon system (20+ lb): $150,000-400,000+
- Ethanol extraction (20-50L): $25,000-80,000
- Ethanol extraction (100L+): $100,000-300,000
- CO2 supercritical extractor (mid-range): $80,000-200,000
- Post-processing: rotary evaporators, vacuum ovens, short-path — add $20,000-80,000

**H3: Safety and Compliance**
- Gas detection system: $5,000-20,000
- Emergency shutoff systems: $3,000-15,000
- PPE and safety equipment: $2,000-10,000
- SOP development and staff training: $5,000-20,000 (or internal)

**H3: Facility Setup**
- Storage: solvent tanks, secondary containment — $5,000-30,000
- Packaging and labeling equipment: $10,000-100,000 depending on volume
- Lab furniture, cleanroom benches: $10,000-40,000

**H2: Total Cost Ranges by Facility Type**
- Small-scale ethanol operation (500-1,000 sqft): $150,000-350,000
- Mid-scale hydrocarbon operation (1,000-2,500 sqft): $400,000-900,000
- Commercial multi-solvent facility (3,000+ sqft): $1,000,000-2,500,000+

**H2: What Drives Costs Up**
- Underestimating C1D1 construction requirements
- Buying equipment before designing the room (leads to retrofits)
- State-specific requirements that exceed federal baseline (California, Colorado, Massachusetts)
- Choosing equipment based on purchase price alone vs. throughput efficiency

**H2: What You Can Phase**
- Phase 1: Core extraction line + C1D1 room, basic post-processing
- Phase 2: Add secondary post-processing, expand throughput
- Phase 3: Packaging automation, additional capacity
