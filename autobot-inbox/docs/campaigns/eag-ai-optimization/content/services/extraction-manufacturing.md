### Cannabis Extraction & Manufacturing Lab Design Consulting

Setting up a cannabis extraction or manufacturing facility is one of the most technically demanding and heavily regulated undertakings in the cannabis industry. The difference between a well-designed facility and a costly buildout mistake often comes down to whether you had experienced guidance before breaking ground — not after.

### What We Do

#### Facility Planning & Layout

A cannabis extraction facility is not a warehouse with machines in it. Every square foot has regulatory implications. We help operators design facilities that are:

- Compliant with state-specific manufacturing regulations
- Operationally efficient from intake through finished product
- Built around the extraction method(s) you're actually using
- Scalable as your production volume grows

We produce detailed facility layouts, process flow diagrams, and zone plans that your architects and contractors can work from directly.

#### C1D1 Room Design & Specification

Class 1 Division 1 (C1D1) classified areas are required for hydrocarbon extraction (BHO, propane, and mixed hydrocarbon) in virtually every regulated cannabis state. A compliant C1D1 room requires:

- HVAC and ventilation designed to prevent flammable vapor accumulation (calculated air changes per hour based on solvent type and volume)
- Electrical systems rated for classified environments (explosion-proof fixtures, conduit, outlets, and panels)
- Gas detection systems with appropriate set points and alarm integration
- Emergency egress that meets both fire code and state cannabis regulations
- Spill containment and solvent storage that satisfies both EPA and state DEA requirements

We specify each of these systems in detail and can coordinate directly with your mechanical and electrical engineers to ensure the design translates to a compliant build.

#### Equipment Selection & Procurement

The extraction equipment market is crowded with vendors making aggressive claims. We provide independent, vendor-neutral equipment evaluation across all major extraction methods:

**Hydrocarbon (BHO/Propane):**
- Closed-loop systems from small batch (1–5 lb) to production scale (50+ lb per run)
- Dewaxing/winterization column configurations
- Solvent recovery and recycling systems
- Vacuum oven specifications for post-processing

**Ethanol:**
- Room-temperature vs. cryogenic ethanol systems
- Centrifuge vs. filter press extraction configurations
- Evaporation and recovery (falling film evaporators, rotary evaporators)
- Throughput planning for high-volume biomass processing

**CO2:**
- Supercritical and subcritical system configurations
- Tuning protocols for strain-specific extract profiles
- Economic comparison vs. hydrocarbon and ethanol at various production scales

**Post-Processing & Refinement:**
- Short-path and wiped-film distillation
- Liquid-liquid separation (LLE)
- Chromatography (flash, HPLC) for remediation and isolation
- Formulation equipment for vape, edible, and tincture production lines

#### Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Regulatory bodies require written SOPs for extraction facilities. We draft complete operational SOPs that cover:

- Daily startup and shutdown procedures
- Extraction run execution
- Solvent inventory management and reconciliation
- Equipment cleaning and maintenance schedules
- Emergency response procedures
- Quality control sampling and testing protocols

SOPs are written to satisfy both state licensing requirements and GMP-adjacent operational standards.

#### Regulatory Compliance Strategy

Cannabis manufacturing regulations vary significantly by state — what's required in Colorado differs from California, Michigan, Illinois, or Oklahoma. We map your facility design to the specific regulatory requirements in your jurisdiction and identify gaps before they become inspection failures or license conditions.
