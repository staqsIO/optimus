### Cannabis Operational Analysis & Auditing

Most cannabis operators know something is wrong — yields are lower than expected, cost per unit is climbing, compliance issues keep surfacing — but don't have an independent, experienced eye to identify the root causes and prioritize fixes.

An operational audit from Elevated Advisory Group gives you a complete, honest assessment of how your cannabis operation performs against industry benchmarks and what's preventing you from performing better.

---

### What a Cannabis Operational Audit Covers

#### Regulatory Compliance

An audit is not an inspection — but it gives you the same visibility a regulator would have, before they do.

We review:
- License conditions vs. actual operating procedures
- Track-and-trace (Metrc or state equivalent) accuracy and reconciliation
- Required SOPs — are they written, current, followed?
- Physical security and surveillance compliance
- Waste disposal documentation and procedures
- Employee training records and verification

Most operators have compliance gaps they don't know about. Finding them through an independent audit is dramatically less costly than finding them during a regulatory inspection.

#### Process Efficiency & Throughput

For extraction and manufacturing operations, we analyze:
- Input-to-output yield ratios by product type and batch
- Equipment utilization rates and downtime causes
- Throughput bottlenecks (where is product waiting longest?)
- Labor allocation vs. production volume
- Solvent recovery and material loss accounting

For cultivation operations:
- Yield per square foot vs. expected range for the cultivar and environment
- Environmental control consistency (temperature, humidity, CO2, VPD)
- Pest and pathogen pressure and response effectiveness
- Clone-to-harvest cycle times and variance

#### Cost Structure Analysis

We calculate your true cost per unit — by SKU, extraction method, or cultivation room — and compare it against industry benchmarks. Common findings:

- Labor is allocated to low-value tasks that should be automated or eliminated
- Testing costs are higher than necessary due to batch sizing or formulation practices
- Utility costs are disproportionate due to environmental control inefficiency
- Packaging and materials costs have not been renegotiated as volume has grown

#### Quality Control

We review your QA/QC procedures and outcomes:
- Batch failure rates and common failure modes
- Testing lab selection and COA review practices
- Product consistency standards and how they're enforced
- Recall procedures and documentation

#### Staffing & Organization

Understaffing creates compliance risk. Overstaffing erodes margins. Misaligned responsibilities create accountability gaps. We evaluate:
- Whether current staffing is appropriate for production volume and license conditions
- Whether roles and responsibilities are clearly defined and understood
- Whether supervisory coverage meets licensing requirements

### What You Get

A written operational audit report with:

1. **Executive summary** — Top 5–10 findings with severity rating (critical, significant, minor)
2. **Detailed findings** — Each finding with documentation, root cause analysis, and recommended corrective action
3. **ROI estimates** — For each recommended improvement, an estimate of the financial or compliance benefit
4. **Prioritized action plan** — Sequenced recommendations based on impact and implementation difficulty

Most operators who complete a formal audit recover the audit cost within 60–90 days through a single implemented finding.

---

### How an Engagement Works

1. **Pre-audit document review** — You share licenses, SOPs, recent test results, and financial summaries. We review before arriving.
2. **On-site audit** — 1–3 days on-site depending on operation size and scope. We observe, interview, and document.
3. **Analysis and report preparation** — 5–10 business days.
4. **Report delivery and debrief** — We walk through findings with you and answer questions.
5. **Implementation support** — Available on retainer as you work through corrective actions.
